Features that I think may be correlated to each other:

I tried taking 6 different features to diversify the analysis.

Temperature Vs Dew Point: The dew point is the temperature at which air becomes so full of moisture that it starts to release it as liquid. It’s tied to how humid the air is. When the air warms up, it can hold more moisture, so the dew point often goes up too.

Humidity vs Rainfall: Both metrics should be correlated as they are typically engaged in a feedback loop with each other. On one had if it rains, more moisture will be released into the air. On the other, hight water content in the air makes it more likely to rain.

Snowfall vs Solar Radiation: Snowfall usually only occurs when days are short and skies are overcast, which means there’s less sunlight and, therefore, less solar radiation.

The results for linear regression are pretty poor for the test data, scoring 0.54 as an R2 score. This means only 54% of the variations in the demande can be explained by the model and it's features. The linear regression model relies on the assumption that there is a linear relationship between the independent features and the rented bike count. In our dataset, only temperature and hour have moderate linear correlations with bike demand, with correlation coefficients of 0.54 and 0.41, respectively. All other features exhibit relatively low linear correlations with the target variable. This means that most features do not contribute significantly to the model's predictive power in a linear context. As a result, the model struggles to accurately predict the bike demand because it cannot capture the underlying patterns effectively with linear relationships alone. The Random Forest model did much better on the test data, with an R2 score of 0.86. This means it can explain 86% of the changes in bike demand based on the features we used. There is some level of overfitting as seen in the increase in MSE compared to the training data but overall the resuls are still good. As stated before, in this dataset, we can't pull out meaningful preditions by just plotting a line through the data. Fortunately Random Forest builds multiple decision trees and can thus "see" more links between the features. Therefore it makes much better prediction an the mean square average is 3x smaller than in linear regression.
