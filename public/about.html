About

My path so far has been a series of pivots driven by curiosity. I spent seven years in financial services, first working on foreclosure data and then moving into bond securitization at a public REIT, where I helped run end-to-end execution of eight bond offerings totaling roughly $3 billion in aggregate issuance. The work was intricate and consequential, but I wanted to move closer to the technology that's reshaping how problems get solved. So I came to NYU Tandon to pursue an M.S. in Computer Science.

Now I'm building things that combine that financial domain with modern AI and cloud systems. I work as a Tech Intern at NYU's Office of Global Services and as a Teacher Assistant for Tandon's graduate Artificial Intelligence course (CS6613). The projects on this site span AI and machine learning, cloud architecture, data visualization, and game design, areas that look unrelated until you start working in them.

I speak English and French fluently, and I'm proficient in Spanish. Based in New York City.
