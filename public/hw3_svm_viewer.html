DoS Detection with SVM Kernels

I trained four SVM models (linear, RBF, sigmoid, polynomial kernels) to detect denial-of-service attacks in the KDD Cup '99 intrusion dataset, then compared how each kernel handles the same data. My full code is in the notebook further down the page. With all 41 features available, three of the four kernels nail it to roughly 100% accuracy and sigmoid trails slightly behind once its gamma is tuned. The more interesting story is in the second part, where I retrain on just two features (dst_host_srv_diff_host_rate and dst_bytes) and plot the decision boundary. Accuracy drops to 92 to 93% and the visualization shows exactly where the linear kernel's flat boundary differs from the RBF kernel's curved one, plus the overlap region that neither model can resolve cleanly.
