Phishing URL Detection with Decision Trees

I trained decision-tree classifiers on a phishing-vs-benign URL dataset (~36k URLs, 79 hand-engineered features), sweeping depths 1 to 6 across Gini and entropy. The classes were 4:1 benign-to-phishing, so I undersampled the training set to 1:1, which pulled benign precision up without hurting phishing recall. A depth-1 stump hits 80%, depth 2 hits 86%, and by depth 6 both criteria converge on 93%. Gini wins the middle of the range (90% vs. 86% at depth 3), consistent with its simpler splits generalizing better as depth grows. The depth-2 tree splits on SymbolCount_Domain at the root, then on urlLen and delimeter_path. Full code and per-depth confusion matrices are in the notebook below.
