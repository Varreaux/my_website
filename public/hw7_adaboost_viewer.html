Phishing URL Detection with AdaBoost

I trained AdaBoost (SAMME, 500 estimators) on the same phishing-vs-benign URL dataset I used for the decision-tree project, this time filtered to only the benign and phishing rows (~15.4k URLs). I swept the base-tree depth across 1, 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18 with both Gini and entropy. The headline: AdaBoost on depth-1 stumps already hits 95% accuracy out of the gate, where the plain decision tree at depth 1 only got 80%. Accuracy peaks at 99% around depths 6 to 12 and starts dropping at depth 15-18 as the base trees overfit. Gini and entropy track each other almost perfectly, with the same accuracy at every depth except 18 (where entropy slips to 97%). The notebook below has per-depth confusion matrices and a side-by-side comparison against the plain decision tree from the previous project.

Try it

Below is a 2D playground that trains real AdaBoost (SAMME) on points you control, using axis-aligned decision stumps as the weak learner. Slide "Boost rounds" to step through the ensemble round by round. The thin gray lines are every stump that has been added so far, and the bold dark line is the most recent one. Point sizes reflect each point's current weight: misclassified points get up-weighted between rounds, so they grow as boosting progresses, which is exactly the mechanism that lets AdaBoost build a curved boundary out of straight cuts. XOR is the canonical test, the boundary needs at least four stumps to lock in.
