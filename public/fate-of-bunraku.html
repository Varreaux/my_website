Fate of Bunraku

A 3D horror / puzzle game.

Click HERE to play the game on itch. It's browser-based, so very easy to play.

A 3D first-person adventure/puzzle game built in Godot 4 for a game design course. It was my first 3D project after working mainly in 2D (roguelikes, strategy, turn-based), and I wanted to explore horror through atmosphere and environmental storytelling.

The game draws from Japanese Bunraku theater and tragic romance, reimagined as horror. The team researched traditional theater structures, themes, and cultural symbolism, then adapted them into a playable experience.

My role covered environmental interactions, object manipulation, and puzzle logic, including mechanics where objects inserted into or extracted from paintings alter world state. I also contributed to level layout, dialogue, voice acting, character concepts, and lore, and gained hands-on experience with 3D assets and meshes.

The main challenge was balance: tension without overwhelming players, readable puzzles that don't break immersion, and lore that rewards curiosity without exposition dumps. This reinforced that code, art, sound, and narrative are interconnected.

As a team project with strong-minded designers, collaboration required clear communication and compromise. I learned to argue constructively, let go when needed, and adapt my work to support a shared vision.

The project confirmed that games are integrated systems and reshaped how I approach development. It also reinforced my interest in exploring unfamiliar genres and tools.
